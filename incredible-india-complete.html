Competition Timeline

Important Dates and Milestones

School Level Competition

November 2025

School-level competitions will be conducted across all participating schools throughout the month of November. Students will showcase their skills in their respective categories with regional judging and evaluation.

  • Registration opens in October
  • Preliminary rounds start early November
  • Finals held mid-November
  • Winners announced by end of November

Zonal Level Competition

December 2025

Zonal-level competitions will be conducted in December where winners from school-level competitions will compete at the zonal level. This provides a platform for talented students to showcase their skills on a broader stage.

  • Top performers from school level qualify
  • Zonal hubs established across regions
  • National-level judges evaluate performance
  • Merit certificates and awards distributed

National Recognition & Awards

January 2026

Outstanding performers from zonal competitions will receive national recognition and awards. This milestone celebrates excellence and provides career advancement opportunities in tourism and hospitality sectors.

  • Grand finale planning begins
  • Selected participants for national event
  • Skill certification programs
  • Industry partnerships and placement opportunities

About the Competition

Empowering Students Through Skill Excellence

Our Mission

The Incredible India: Heritage, Hospitality & Tourism Skill Competition aims to showcase India's rich cultural heritage and world-class hospitality standards. By engaging students in practical skill demonstrations, we nurture the next generation of industry professionals who will represent India on the global tourism stage.

Our Vision

To create a platform where young talents from across India can demonstrate their skills in heritage preservation, hospitality excellence, and tourism innovation. We envision a generation equipped with both traditional knowledge and modern technological expertise to elevate India's tourism sector.

Competition Objectives

  • Skill Development: Encourage students to develop practical skills in heritage, hospitality, and tourism sectors
  • Industry Exposure: Connect students with industry professionals and career opportunities
  • Cultural Preservation: Promote traditional knowledge and practices in modern contexts
  • Quality Service: Establish standards for excellence in tourism and hospitality services
  • Innovation: Encourage creative solutions for tourism challenges using technology

Competition Structure

The competition operates on a two-tier system, beginning with school-level competitions in November, where students compete with peers in their regions. Winners then advance to the zonal-level competition in December, where they compete at a broader stage. This structure ensures equal opportunity and fair evaluation of all participants.

Travel Tips & Best Time to Visit

Practical tips for participants and visitors planning to attend events or explore India's heritage sites.

  • Best Time: October to March ‚Äî pleasant weather across most regions.
  • Packing: Lightweight cottons for day, a light jacket for cool evenings, and comfortable walking shoes.
  • Local Travel: Use registered taxis or app-based services; book trains and long-distance travel in advance.
  • Health & Safety: Carry basic medicines, stay hydrated, and practice food-hygiene when trying street foods.
  • Cultural Etiquette: Dress respectfully at religious sites and ask permission before photographing people.
